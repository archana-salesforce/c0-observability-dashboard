    Appendix / Heuristics

    View rubric and metric definitions
    ToneHow natural, credible, and appropriate the email sounds for outreach.
    PersonalizationHow specifically the email reflects the lead's role, company, or context.
    CTAHow clearly and strongly the email asks for a next step or meeting.
    AccuracyWhether the email is factually correct and avoids misleading statements.
    ClarityHow easy the message is to read and understand.
    RelevanceHow well the content connects the value proposition to likely lead needs.
    PersonalityHow well the email reflects urgency and meeting-oriented SDR behavior.
    Latest group assignmentLead-level experiment flow is resolved by earliest observed assignment. 9 leads appear in both groups in the raw export.
    Treatment regenerated leadTreatment lead with at least one RECORD_TYPE = regenerated row. Only treatment leads can be coached.
    Selection Rate Among FailuresOf treatment leads whose earliest observable initial draft fails a rubric category, the share that later receive a regenerated email. Higher values mean the coach is more likely to step in when that category fails.
    Coached sampleThe current comparison sample is the matched treatment subset used in Coaching Impact. It includes regenerated drafts with an observable initial draft and excludes regenerated drafts with no initial draft to compare against.
    Regeneration score liftAverage regenerated score minus average matched initial score for the same treatment lead. Positive values mean the coach improved that rubric dimension.
    Meeting booked rate deltaAbsolute difference in meeting booked rate from asdr-metrics.csv using that file's own group assignments. Current read: treatment 1.12% versus control 1.01%, or +0.10 percentage points.
    Example EmailsExamples are drawn from the selected category and show the largest observed score gain after coaching in the current export.
